## LinkedIn

Most founders I talk to are not bad with money. They are just out of time.

They can tell you their best month and their worst client off the top of their head. Ask them if they were profitable last month and you get a pause, then a guess, then a slightly embarrassed laugh.

That pause is the whole problem. Not the math. The not-knowing.

Here is what changes when the books are clean and current:

- You stop guessing. You open one short summary and you know.
- Sunday stops disappearing into a pile of receipts.
- Tax season turns into a forwarded email instead of a panic.
- You make decisions on real numbers instead of the balance in your checking account.

You do not need to become an accountant. You need to hand the books to a real person who keeps them clean and tells you, in plain English, where you stand.

Behind on your books? That is the normal place to start. Most of our founders came in months behind and braced for a lecture. They got caught up instead.

Clean books, by the 10th, every month. That is the entire promise.

What is the longest you have gone without knowing if you were profitable? No judgment. I have heard "two years" and did not flinch.
